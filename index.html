About

I'm a cybersecruity engineer. I hold a Master's degeree in Cybersecurity and a Bachelopr's in Computer Science. I'm also a bit of a nerd and love to learn new things about technology and gadgets. While I may not have a conventional professional experience, my internship and my projects in the field have provided me with a strong understanding of cybersecurity foundations and hands-on experience in network analysis, vulnerability management, and incident response. I'm always looking for new challenges, and I'm excited to see what the future holds for cybersecurity. I believe that cybersecurity is one of the most important fields in the world today, and I'm committed to making a difference.

I'm proficient in a variety of security tools and technologies, including Nessus, Qualys, SIEM, Splunk, Orca Security, Autopsy, Burpsuite, Metasploit, MITRE ATT&CK, Penetration Testing, Threat Assessment, AWS, Linux, Docker, Git, OAuth, LDAP, IAM, Azure, Cloud Infrastructure, TCP/IP, DNS. I'm an active participant in HacktheBox and TryHackMe labs, where I have completed over 50 challenges. I have a strong understanding of penetration testing techniques and I'm able to identify and exploit vulnerabilities in a variety of systems.
In my spare time, I maintain a home lab environment for security testing, threat hunting, and incident response techniques, leveraging tools like Metasploit, Tenable, Nessus, and Nmap. I am also a member of WiCys, Graduate Women in Engineering, Game Development Club, and Cybersecurity Club at UMD. Looking forward, I am pursuing the CompTIA Security+ certification and have completed the Jr.Penetration Tester route in TryHackMe to continue developing my skills in cloud security, penetration testing, and other areas to contribute to the ever-evolving cybersecurity industry.
